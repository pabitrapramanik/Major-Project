About West Bengal

Experience the cultural capital of India with its rich heritage and diverse landscapes

The Cultural Heart of India

West Bengal, located in Eastern India, is a state blessed with natural splendor and cultural richness. From the snow-capped Himalayas in the north to the Bay of Bengal in the south, this state offers diverse experiences to travelers.

Kolkata (formerly Calcutta), the capital city, is known as the cultural capital of India. The state boasts a rich heritage of art, literature, music, and cinema, owing much to the Bengal Renaissance of the 19th century.

With its delectable cuisine, vibrant festivals, magnificent architecture, picturesque hill stations, and pristine beaches, West Bengal promises an unforgettable journey through the heart of Bengali culture and tradition.

Culture & Heritage

Discover the rich cultural tapestry that makes West Bengal unique

🎭

Arts & Literature

Home to Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore and countless literary giants, West Bengal has been the epicenter of art, literature, and intellectual discourse in India.

🎊

Festivals

Experience the grandeur of Durga Puja, the biggest festival of Bengal, along with other colorful celebrations like Poush Mela, Gangasagar Mela, and many more.

🍲

Cuisine

Savor the delectable Bengali cuisine known for its subtle flavors, fish preparations, sweets like Rasgulla and Sandesh, and the unique five-spice blend called Panch Phoron.

🎵

Music & Dance

From classical Rabindra Sangeet to folk traditions like Baul music and dance forms like Chhau and Kathak, West Bengal offers a rich performing arts heritage.

Travel Tips

Essential information for planning your trip to West Bengal

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the ideal time to visit West Bengal. The weather is pleasant and most of the festivals take place during this period. Summers (April to June) are hot and humid, while monsoons (July to September) bring heavy rainfall.

How to Reach

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata connects the state to major cities in India and abroad. West Bengal is well-connected by railway networks and roadways. The state also has waterways connecting to Bangladesh.

Local Transportation

In Kolkata, you can use the metro, trams, buses, auto-rickshaws, and the iconic yellow taxis. In other parts of the state, buses, shared jeeps, and cycle rickshaws are common modes of transportation.

Accommodation

From luxury hotels to budget guesthouses, West Bengal offers a wide range of accommodation options. In Kolkata, you'll find international hotel chains, while hill stations like Darjeeling offer charming colonial-era properties and homestays.
