About Maharashtra

Experience the cultural capital of India with its rich heritage and diverse landscapes

The Land of Cultural Heritage of India

Maharashtra, located in western India, is a state of diverse landscapes and rich heritage. From the Sahyadri mountains to the Konkan coastline, it offers a blend of history, spirituality, and modernity.

Mumbai, the bustling capital, is India’s financial hub and home to Bollywood. The state is known for its UNESCO-listed Ajanta and Ellora Caves, majestic forts like Raigad and Sinhagad, spiritual sites like Shirdi and Trimbakeshwar, and scenic hill stations like Lonavala and Mahabaleshwar.

With its delectable cuisine, grand festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi, and a legacy of Maratha valor, Maharashtra is a vibrant destination that captures India’s essence.

Culture & Heritage

Discover the rich cultural tapestry that makes Maharashtra unique

🎭

Arts & Literature

Maharashtra has a rich artistic and literary heritage, with luminaries like P. L. Deshpande and Kusumagraj shaping Marathi literature. The state is also home to vibrant Warli and Paithani art traditions.

🎊

Festivals

Celebrate Maharashtra’s grand Ganesh Chaturthi, along with other vibrant festivals like Gudi Padwa, Ellora Festival, and Narali Purnima, showcasing the state’s cultural richness.

🍲

Cuisine

Indulge in Maharashtra’s diverse cuisine, from spicy Misal Pav and Vada Pav to coastal delicacies like Malvani fish curry and the sweet delights of Puran Poli and Modak..

🎵

Music & Dance

Maharashtra boasts a rich musical heritage, including Lavani folk dance, Natya Sangeet (theater music), and classical Hindustani music, with legends like Bhimsen Joshi hailing from the state.

Travel Tips

Essential information for planning your trip to Maharashtra

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the ideal time to visit Maharashtra, as the weather is pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Summers (April to June) are hot, especially in cities like Mumbai and Nagpur, while monsoons (July to September) bring heavy rainfall, enhancing the beauty of hill stations like Lonavala and Mahabaleshwar.

How to Reach

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport in Mumbai connects Maharashtra to major cities in India and abroad. The state has a vast railway network and excellent road connectivity. Additionally, Maharashtra has major ports like Mumbai Port and Jawaharlal Nehru Port for water transport.

Local Transportation

Mumbai has an extensive local train network, metro, buses, auto-rickshaws, and iconic black-and-yellow taxis. Pune, Nashik, and other cities offer buses, auto-rickshaws, and app-based cabs, while hill stations have shared jeeps and private taxis.

Accommodation

Maharashtra offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels in Mumbai and Pune to budget lodges and homestays in smaller towns and hill stations. Popular tourist destinations like Lonavala, Mahabaleshwar, and Ajanta-Ellora have resorts and guesthouses catering to different budgets.
